Data Privacy Policy

Axiom Retention Group · Last updated: 14 May 2026 · Version 1.0
Summary for clients: We work exclusively with anonymised member IDs. We do not collect names, emails, payment details, or any other personally identifiable information. Your members' data never leaves a secure, encrypted environment and is deleted within 30 days of report delivery unless a formal service agreement is in place.
Section 01

Who We Are

Axiom Retention Group ("Axiom", "we", "us", "our") provides data analytics and churn prediction services for fitness businesses in the United Kingdom. Our registered business contact is listed in Section 9 of this policy.

Under UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018, Axiom Retention Group acts as a Data Processor when handling client-supplied member data. The fitness business engaging our services is the Data Controller and retains responsibility for its members' personal data. This distinction matters: your obligations to your members under UK GDPR are not transferred to us by virtue of this engagement.

Section 02

Data We Process

To perform a churn analysis, we request the minimum dataset necessary. We specifically instruct clients to export anonymised records only. The table below describes what we process and our lawful basis for doing so.

Data type Example Required Purpose
Anonymised member ID Member_12345 Yes Links attendance records without identifying individuals

If you send us a file containing personally identifiable information in error, we will notify you promptly, delete the data without processing it, and request a corrected export.

Section 03

How We Use the Data

Data provided to Axiom is used solely for the agreed analytical purpose. Specifically:

  • Modelling attendance patterns and visit frequency decay to identify behavioural churn signals.
  • Generating per-member churn probability scores and risk-tier classifications (CRITICAL, HIGH, ELEVATED, MONITOR).
  • Identifying the primary behavioural drivers of churn risk for reporting purposes.
  • Producing revenue-at-risk projections and recovery estimates for executive reporting.

We do not use your data for any other commercial purpose, do not sell or share it with third parties, and do not use it to train generalised models that are deployed outside your engagement.

Section 04

Lawful Basis for Processing

As a Data Processor acting under instruction from a Data Controller, Axiom processes data on the basis of a contractual service agreement. The Data Controller (you, the client) is responsible for ensuring your own lawful basis for sharing member data with a third-party analytics provider — typically legitimate interests under UK GDPR Article 6(1)(f), given that churn analysis is a core operational activity directly in members' interests (retaining a service they value).

We recommend consulting your own legal counsel if you are uncertain of your lawful basis. We are happy to assist with the legitimate interests assessment (LIA) documentation on request.

Section 05

Security & Storage

  • Encryption in transit: All data transfers use TLS 1.2 or higher. We do not accept data via unencrypted channels (plain email attachments are discouraged; secure transfer links are provided on request).
  • Encryption at rest: Data stored on cloud infrastructure (AWS S3) is encrypted using AES-256 server-side encryption.
  • Access control: Access to raw datasets is restricted to the Lead Data Scientist conducting the audit. No sub-processors or third-party tools have access to identifiable member records.
  • Infrastructure: Our platform runs on AWS infrastructure located in the EU-West (London) region. Data does not leave the UK or EEA.
  • No third-party sharing: We do not pass client data to advertising platforms, analytics trackers, or any other commercial third party.
Section 06

Data Retention & Deletion

Our retention schedule is as follows:

  • One-off Leakage Audit: Raw input data is deleted within 30 days of report delivery. Aggregated, non-identifiable outputs (model performance statistics, anonymised summary metrics) may be retained for internal benchmarking.
  • Ongoing service agreement: Data is retained for the duration of the active service agreement and deleted within 30 days of agreement termination, unless a longer retention period is agreed in writing.
  • Right to erasure: You may request deletion of your data at any time by contacting us. We will confirm deletion within 5 business days.
Section 07

Your Rights Under UK GDPR

As the Data Controller, you have the following rights in respect of data held by Axiom on your behalf. You may also need to facilitate these rights for your own members under your separate controller obligations.

  • Right of access: You may request a summary of what data we hold relating to your engagement at any time.
  • Right to rectification: If data was supplied in error, we will correct or replace it on request.
  • Right to erasure: You may request deletion of all data associated with your engagement at any time.
  • Right to restrict processing: You may ask us to pause processing while a query is resolved.
  • Right to data portability: We will provide your input data and any derived outputs in a machine-readable format (CSV or JSON) on request.
  • Right to object: You may object to any specific processing activity. We will cease that activity unless we have compelling legitimate grounds to continue.

To exercise any of these rights, contact us using the details in Section 9. We will respond within 30 days in line with UK GDPR requirements.

Section 08

Data Processing Agreement

Axiom Retention Group is willing to enter into a formal Data Processing Agreement (DPA) with any client prior to data transfer. A DPA is a contractual document that formally records the roles, obligations, and safeguards of each party under UK GDPR Article 28.

If your organisation requires a signed DPA before sharing attendance data — whether for your own compliance purposes or at the request of your members — please contact us before beginning an engagement. We will provide a standard DPA template within 2 business days.

Clients operating under an existing DPA with Axiom should refer to that document in the event of any conflict with the terms stated here; the DPA takes precedence.

Section 09

Data Breach Procedure

In the unlikely event of a data breach affecting client data held by Axiom, we will:

  • Notify you as the Data Controller within 72 hours of becoming aware of the incident, in line with UK GDPR Article 33.
  • Provide a written incident report describing the nature of the breach, the data affected, likely consequences, and remedial measures taken.
  • Cooperate fully with any investigation by the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) if required.

As Data Controller, you are responsible for assessing whether the breach requires notification to the ICO or to affected individuals. We will provide all information necessary to support that assessment.
